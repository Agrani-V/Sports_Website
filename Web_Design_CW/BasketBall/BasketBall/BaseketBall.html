Basketball

What is Basketball?

Basketball is a team sport that is played on a rectangular court with two opposing teams, each consisting of five players. The objective of the game is to score points by shooting a ball through a hoop that is 10 feet (3.048 meters) high and 18 inches (45.7 cm) in diameter, which is attached to a backboard at each end of the cou.

History

Basketball is a popular sport that was invented by Dr. James Naismith in December 1891 in Springfield, Massachusetts, United States. Naismith, a Canadian physical education instructor, was trying to create a new game that could be played indoors during the winter months to keep his students active. The first game of basketball was played with a soccer ball and two peach baskets nailed to the balcony of the gymnasium. The game was initially played with nine players on each team, but this was later reduced to five players per team. The objective of the game was to score points by throwing the ball into the opponent's basket, while the opposing team tried to prevent this from happening.
